Legal Crime

Written at thirteen, Legal Crime is a coming‑of‑age psychological thriller that captures the raw intensity of adolescence — the pressure to fit in, the fear of standing out, and the danger of running from the truth.

The story follows sixteen‑year‑old Fiona Watson, who runs away from home believing she can escape the expectations crushing her. But the world outside is not the freedom she imagined. As Fiona navigates unfamiliar streets, fractured friendships, and the consequences of impulsive decisions, she becomes entangled in secrets that force her to confront who she really is — and who she is becoming.

Beneath the surface of a seemingly ordinary teenage life lies a darker reality shaped by manipulation, hidden motives, and the quiet violence of growing up too fast. Legal Crime explores:

  • • the weight of peer pressure
  • • the search for identity
  • • the danger of secrets
  • • the cost of independence
  • • the moment innocence fractures

Upcoming Novel: Perfected Image (Working Title)

Perfected Image is a dystopian psychological thriller set in the year 2323, where perfection is not an aspiration — it is the law.

The world is divided into towering, walled CitySpaces, each governed by the Perfect Code, a doctrine that dictates everything from appearance to behaviour to emotional expression. At sixteen, every teenager must undergo the Perfection Trials — a ritual that determines their future. Success grants status. Failure leads to disappearance.

Officially, those who fail are sent to Correction Centres for rehabilitation. Unofficially… no one who enters ever returns the same.

In this world of sterile beauty and silent fear, five teenagers begin to experience something the State cannot predict, measure, or control. Their abilities are rare. Illegal. And deeply dangerous. Together, they form SKEAM — a group bound not by choice, but by the secrets they carry and the truth they begin to uncover.

Perfected Image explores:

  • • a society obsessed with perfection
  • • the quiet violence of a system that treats people as assets
  • • the erasure of family, memory, and identity
  • • the power of the “imperfect” — the unpredictable, the emotional, the human
  • • a forgotten history buried beneath the official narrative

The world of Perfected Image is one of gleaming white cities, silent corridors, and secrets hidden behind immaculate smiles. A place where silence is safety — and questions are dangerous.

Perfection is a lie.
Imperfection is rebellion.
The Harvest is coming.

About Samiksha

Samiksha Bhattacharjee is a Gen Z British author and social commentator. She began writing Legal Crime at age seven and published it at thirteen. She hopes to inspire other young people to write boldly, think independently, and challenge the narratives shaping their world.

She is also the host of Samiksha’s State of the Debate, a current‑affairs show promoting free speech and exploring the cultural and political issues impacting young people today.
